## What is the 3% Rule for Sleep Apnea?

The **3% rule** refers to oxygen desaturation events used in sleep apnea diagnosis. An apnea or hypopnea event counts toward your Apnea-Hypopnea Index (AHI) if it causes a 3% or greater drop in blood oxygen levels.

### Sleep Apnea Severity Classifications:
- **Normal**: AHI < 5 events/hour
- **Mild**: AHI 5-15 events/hour  
- **Moderate**: AHI 15-30 events/hour
- **Severe**: AHI > 30 events/hour

The 3% threshold ensures clinically significant events are counted while filtering out minor, non-pathological breathing variations. Some labs also use 4% oxygen desaturation thresholds depending on specific protocols.

## Red Flags: When NOT to Use DTC Sleep Testing

Direct-to-consumer testing isn't appropriate for everyone. Avoid DTC services if you have:

- **Complex medical conditions**: Heart failure, neuromuscular disorders, chronic opioid use
- **Multiple sleep concerns**: Suspected narcolepsy, REM behavior disorder, chronic insomnia
- **Previous inconclusive testing**: Failed CPAP trials, complex sleep histories
- **Age extremes**: Children under 18 or adults over 75 with multiple comorbidities

These situations require comprehensive sleep medicine evaluation and lab-based polysomnography.
